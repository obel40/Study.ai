Protects the freedoms of religion, speech, the press, assembly, and the right to petition the government. 
Protects the individual right to keep and bear arms. 
Prohibits the government from forcing citizens to quarter soldiers in their homes during peacetime. 
Protects citizens from unreasonable searches and seizures by requiring warrants based on probable cause. 
Provides protections against self-incrimination, double jeopardy, and guarantees due process of law. 
Guarantees the right to a speedy and public trial by an impartial jury, the right to confront witnesses, and the right to legal counsel. 
Prohibits excessive bail, excessive fines, and cruel and unusual punishments. 
States that the specific rights listed in the Constitution do not deny or disparage other unrecognized rights retained by the people. 
Grants citizenship to anyone born or naturalized in the U.S. and guarantees equal protection and due process under state laws. 
Abolished slavery and involuntary servitude in the United States, except as punishment for a crime. 
Guaranteed equal protection of the laws and citizenship rights to formerly enslaved individuals. 
Prohibited the government from denying a citizen the right to vote based on that citizen's race, color, or previous condition of servitude. 
Granted women the constitutional right to vote. 
Lowered the national voting age from 21 to 18. 
Granted residents of Washington, D.C., the right to vote in presidential elections by giving the district Electoral College votes.
